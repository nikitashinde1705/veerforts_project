A fort is a military structure designed to defend a territory against attacks, characterized by a strong, fortified area surrounded by walls. The term "fort" comes from the Latin word for "strong". Historically, forts have been built in various terrains using local resources and contemporary technology, serving not just as military outposts but also as residential areas, palaces, and trading centers. Examples in India include the ancient hill fort of Sinhagad near Pune and the Mahim Fort in Mumbai, which has seen action between Portuguese and British forces.Forts are typically surrounded by strong walls, ramparts, or other barriers to repel enemies.
