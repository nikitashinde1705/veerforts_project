Sindhudurg Fort

Sindhudurg Fort is an imposing maritime fortress located on an island in the Arabian Sea, just off the coast of Malvan in the Konkan region of Maharashtra, India. It was built by the legendary Maratha warrior-king, Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, in 1664-1667. Sindhudurg Fort was constructed as a strategic stronghold to protect Maratha territory from the growing influence of the Portuguese, Dutch, English, and Siddi pirates who operated along the western coast of India. Murud-Janjira Fort

Murud-Janjira Fort, also known simply as Janjira Fort, is a majestic island fortress situated off the coastal town of Murud in the Raigad district of Maharashtra, India. Renowned for its unique architectural design and unwavering resilience, Janjira Fort has proudly stood the test of time, earning its reputation as one of India‚Äôs most formidable coastal fortifications.

History and Significance

The origins of Janjira Fort can be traced back to the 14th century when it served as a stronghold for the Siddi dynasty, a powerful naval force of African descent. Despite numerous sieges and battles, it never fell under any invading power, thanks to its strategic location and valiant defenders.
